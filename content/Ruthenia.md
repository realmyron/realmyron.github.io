Ruthenia is an exonym that refers to the core region of the Kyivan Rus. It includes all of Belarus, Northern and Western Ukraine, Smolensk, Bryansk, Kursk and parts of Poland. It's the region of indigenous East Slavic territory. 

# Exonym & Confusion
Until Austria-Hungary, the term "Ruthenia" was just a synonym of Rus & Russia. However, while the meaning was the same, the terms was used by different people, and the term usage is eventually what led to the creation of separate ethnicities. Today, we use the exonym Ruthenia to differentiate from other territories of Rus like Novgorod, Moscow & Don. 

# Endonym
In the 13th century, the East Slavs in Ruthenia began gradually developing an endonym for themselves - Rusyns (Ruthenians). Prior to that, most slavs were calling themselves "Russkiy" (Russian; adjective) which translated to Orthodox
