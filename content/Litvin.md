The Litvin identity is one of the key concepts of Litvinism. It posits that "Litvin" was an identity adopted by Ruthenians, which evolved into an ethnic identity.
___
# Definition & Importance
**Litvin** is an East Slavic word that translates to "Lithuanian." It originates from the 13th century and was originally used as a national title by East Slavs (for example, "I'm from Lithuania so I'm a Litvin") in the GDL. Over time, it slowly evolved into an ethnic identity.

The reason this debate is important, is because many documents regarding the GDL were from the outsiders, which simply used "Lithuanian", which could encompass both lietuva (non-slavs) and litvins (slavs). By defining litvin as a word that refers to only non-slavs, historians can draw a direct continuation path -> **lithuanian (pre-16th century) = lithuanian (modern). **

However, if we prove litvin was an east slavic identity, any document that uses the word lithuanian, will have this continuation path -> lithuanian = lithuanian (modern) & belarus (modern).
